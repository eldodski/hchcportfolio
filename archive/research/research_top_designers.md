## COMMON PATTERNS ACROSS TOP DESIGNERS

### Portfolio Essentials
1. **Photography is everything.** Magazine-quality architectural photography. Without it, nothing else matters.
2. **Fewer projects, shown deeply.** 10-20 projects with 8-15+ images each. Depth over breadth.
3. **Each project tells a story.** Context -- the brief, challenge, design intent, materials. Narrative transforms gallery into portfolio.
4. **Curation IS the pitch.** What you leave out signals confidence as much as what you include.

### Visual Presentation
1. **Full-bleed hero imagery.** Homepage = near-full-screen breathtaking image. No tiny thumbnails.
2. **Clean grid layouts (2-3 columns)** for project indexes. Generous whitespace. Hover effects reveal project names.
3. **Deep project pages.** Each project gets its own page -- wide shots, detail shots, material close-ups, lifestyle moments.
4. **Consistent photography style.** Same photographer or similar style across all projects. Natural light. Warm, lived-in styling.
5. **Minimal text overlay.** Let the work speak. Text is secondary.

### Standard Website Sections

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Home** | Hero image/video, immediate emotional impact, 1-2 sentence positioning |
| **Portfolio/Projects** | Grid of projects, filterable by type (residential, hospitality, commercial) |
| **About/Studio** | Personal story, design philosophy, team. Humanizes the brand. |
| **Services** | What you offer, how the process works |
| **Press/Publications** | Logos of publications featured in (AD, Elle Decor, Vogue, etc.) |
| **Shop/Product** | Many top designers sell furniture, accessories, or curated goods |
| **Journal/Blog** | Design insights, behind-the-scenes, process. SEO driver. |
| **Contact/Inquire** | Clean form. "Inquire" > "Get a Quote" (signals exclusivity) |
| **Newsletter** | Prominent placement, sometimes in top nav |

### Social Media Strategies
1. **Instagram is primary.** Treat it as client-facing portfolio, not peer-impressing highlight reel.
2. **Pinterest is the long-game SEO play.** Pins gain traction for months/years. Pin 3-5x daily.
3. **Content repurposing is systematic.** One project → multiple blog posts, videos, social posts.
4. **Behind-the-scenes and process content performs.** Before/after, material selection, site visits.
5. **Consistency beats virality.** Daily posting, consistent visual style, reliable content calendar.
6. **Video is non-negotiable.** Reels, room tours, time-lapse transformations, BTS.
7. **Posting volume:** Instagram 4-7/week + daily stories | Pinterest 3-5/day | YouTube 1-2/month | Blog 2-4/month

### Branding Elements
- **Photography:** Natural light, warm tones, lifestyle moments alongside architectural shots, texture close-ups
- **Brand colors:** Overwhelmingly neutral (whites, creams, warm beiges). Black backgrounds for ultra-luxury. 2025: "Swiss Coffee" off-whites, Mocha Mousse warmth, dark navy + gold for prestige
- **Typography:** Clean sans-serif dominates. Some pair bold sans-serif with elegant serif. Oversized type trending. Minimal overall.
- **Logo:** Simple wordmark (name in distinctive typeface). Very few use icons. Personal brand > corporate identity.

### Value Proposition Communication
1. **Show, don't tell.** Work does the heavy lifting.
2. **Social proof through press logos.** "As featured in AD, Vogue, Elle Decor..."
3. **Exclusivity signals.** "Inquire" not "Get a Quote." No pricing. Application-style intake.
4. **Personal narrative on About page.** A story, not a resume.
5. **Books and product lines as credibility markers.**
6. **Selective process transparency.** Show enough to educate on value without giving away methodology.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR BUILDING A PORTFOLIO

1. **Invest in photography first.** Get 5-10 projects shot by a professional architectural photographer with consistent style.
2. **Website = 80% images, 20% text.** Clean, spacious, fast-loading. Squarespace is standard (9 of top portfolios use it).
3. **Pick a lane on style.** Most successful designers have instantly recognizable aesthetic. Specificity beats versatility in branding.
4. **Instagram = second portfolio.** Post daily, use for client acquisition not peer validation, show process + finished work.
5. **Build beyond the portfolio.** Top tier all have 2+ of: product line, book, retail, media/TV, blog/editorial. Portfolio opens the door; ecosystem builds the empire.
