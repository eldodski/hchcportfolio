## IDENTIFIED SIGNATURE STYLE

### The Style Lane: **"Warm Transitional"**
Ena's work sits squarely in the **transitional** zone -- the sweet spot between traditional warmth and contemporary clean lines. This is the exact same territory as **Shea McGee / Studio McGee**, the most commercially successful designer brand in the industry.

### Core Design DNA

**1. Warm Neutral Base**
Every project starts with a foundation of warm neutrals -- taupes, creams, warm grays, sand tones. Walls are never stark white; they're warm. Floors are always warm-toned wood (or wood-look).

**2. Navy Blue as Signature Accent**
Navy appears as THE consistent bold color choice across multiple projects -- navy pillows (Gaffney, Leek), navy accent chair (Ukeiley), navy dining chairs (formal dining), and most dramatically as the ENTIRE lower cabinet + island color in the kitchen renovation. This is a signature.

**3. Rich Wood Tones**
Walnut, dark oak, salvaged timber -- warm wood shows up in every project. Tables, credenzas, bar stools, pendant lights, beams. She clearly loves the warmth that real/wood-tone materials bring.

**4. Texture Mixing**
Consistently layers textures: linen upholstery + leather chairs + woven rugs + marble surfaces + wood grain. Rarely flat or monotone -- there's always tactile variety.

**5. Pattern Play Through Rugs + Pillows**
Bold patterned area rugs are a consistent element (medallion, geometric, floral, Persian). She uses rugs and pillows as the primary pattern-injection point, keeping larger pieces more neutral.

**6. Traditional Bones, Modern Execution**
The silhouettes nod to traditional (pedestal tables, upholstered dining chairs, etagere shelving), but the overall composition reads modern and clean. She doesn't go full-farmhouse or full-minimalist -- she threads the needle.

**7. Livability First**
Every room looks like someone actually lives there. The guitar in the living room, the cookbooks in the island, the personal art choices. She designs FOR the client's life, not for a magazine spread.

**8. Statement Lighting**
Orb chandeliers, sculptural wood pendants, arc floor lamps, industrial desk lamps. Lighting fixtures are consistently used as design focal points.

## BRAND BOOK IMPLICATIONS

### Style Positioning Statement (Draft)
*"Warm, layered interiors that feel both curated and lived-in. Timeless design grounded in rich textures, bold navy accents, and the warmth of natural wood."*

### Key Differentiators to Build On
1. **The renovation transformation story** -- before/after is the most compelling content type in interior design
2. **Navy as a signature** -- own it explicitly in the brand (not many designers claim a specific color the way India Mahdavi claims pink)
3. **Approachable luxury** -- high-end look at accessible price points (the Bassett partnership proves this)
4. **Whole-home design** -- not just "pick a sofa," but complete environments

### Biggest Portfolio Gaps (Honest Assessment)
1. **Photography quality** -- the existing images are phone photos and design-tool mockups, not professional architectural photography. This is the #1 investment needed.
2. **No staged/styled "after" shots** -- the kitchen reno photos are good but taken during/right after construction (boxes visible, not styled). Professional shoot of that kitchen alone would transform the portfolio.
3. **Design presentations** -- the Bassett boards are functional but branded to Bassett, not to Ena. Needs own branded presentation format.
4. **Limited project variety visible** -- kitchen reno is the hero, but needs more completed + photographed projects to show range.
