03 — Photography & Imagery

Photography & Imagery

Photography is the single most important brand asset for an interior designer. It's not optional — it's the product.

Photography Style

ElementGuideline
LightingNatural light preferred. Warm color temperature. Golden hour or overcast. Never harsh flash.
TemperatureWarm (5500–6500K). Never cool/blue.
CompositionWide establishing (40%) + medium vignettes (30%) + tight close-ups (30%)
StylingLived-in but curated. Books, flowers, candles, coffee cup. Never sterile, never cluttered.
EditingMinimal. Light and airy processing. No heavy filters or desaturation.
PerspectiveEye-level for living spaces. Straight-on for kitchens/built-ins. No extreme wide-angle distortion.

Shot List (Per Project)

  1. 2–3 wide establishing shots (different angles)
  2. 2–3 medium vignette shots (styled corners, furniture groupings)
  3. 2–3 detail/texture close-ups (countertop grain, fabric, hardware, tile)
  4. 1 lifestyle shot (someone using the space naturally)
  5. 1–2 architectural detail shots (lighting fixture, molding, built-in)

Total: 8–14 images per project minimum.

Before & After Rules

  • Same angle, same lens for before and after. Non-negotiable for impact.
  • Before shots: honest but well-lit enough to read clearly
  • After shots: fully styled and professionally lit
  • Always pair them — never show an after without context

Photography Investment Priority

Immediate: Reshoot the Kitchen

Hire a professional architectural photographer to reshoot the completed navy kitchen renovation. Styled, same-angle before/after. This single project, properly photographed, is worth more than 10 phone photos.
Budget: $500–$1,500 for a half-day shoot.

Imagery Don'ts

  • No phone photos in the portfolio (BTS social content is fine from phone)
  • No photos with construction debris, boxes, or visible brand tags
  • No heavily filtered or artificially saturated images
  • No stock photography on the website — ever
  • No Bassett/HGTV-branded presentation boards in the portfolio
04 — Brand Voice & Messaging

Brand Voice

Voice Traits

Warm
Approachable, friendly, conversational. Not stiff or formal.
"Let's talk about your space" — not "Submit an inquiry regarding our services"
Confident
Knows her stuff. States opinions clearly. Not wishy-washy. 8+ years backs every recommendation.
"Navy is always the right answer" — not "Navy might be a nice option if you're considering it"
Strategic
Thinks beyond aesthetics — about timelines, budgets, builder relationships, and business impact. Design decisions are grounded in ROI.
"Upgrading these three selections adds $12K in perceived value for under $3K in material cost"
Professional
Speaks the language of builders and trades, not just Pinterest boards. Delivers on time, communicates proactively, runs a tight process.
"Here's the selection schedule aligned to your build timeline" — not "We'll figure it out as we go"

Do's & Don'ts

Do
  • Use "we" and "your" — it's a partnership
  • Reference specific experience and expertise
  • Use specific details (material names, color references, trade names)
  • Be willing to have a design opinion — that's what they're hiring you for
  • Speak like you're talking to a smart friend, not a client file
Don't
  • Be self-deprecating or hedging ("I think maybe this could work?")
  • Use jargon without context ("bespoke millwork solution" → "custom built-ins")
  • Use corporate buzzwords ("leverage," "synergy," "optimize")
  • Use flowery clichés ("sanctuary of serenity")
  • Apologize for having opinions — clients want decisiveness

Page Guidelines

Home

  • Hero: Single full-bleed image + "Warm by Design." + CTA "View Our Work"
  • 3–4 portfolio preview images in a clean grid below the fold
  • 2–3 sentences of positioning copy
  • One strong client testimonial
  • CTA: "Ready to transform your space?"

Portfolio

  • 2–3 column grid, generous whitespace, warm hover zoom effect
  • Each project: 8–14 images, 1–2 sentence brief, scope tags, before/after
  • Let images carry the narrative — minimal text

Services

  • Builder Partnerships: Design program development, curated selection packages, design center setup/management, client-facing appointments. Position this FIRST — it's the differentiator.
  • Residential Design: Full-service for custom builds and renovations. Process: Discovery → Concept → Selections → Install → Reveal
  • Pre-Listing Refresh: Targeted updates (paint, fixtures, hardware, staging) for realtors. Fast turnaround, high ROI.
  • No pricing on the website. CTA: "Let's Talk" or "Start a Project"
  • Include a "For Realtors" section highlighting pre-listing refresh services and fast turnaround

Journal / Blog

  • 2–4 posts/month — the SEO engine
  • Topics: design tips, project BTS, material spotlights, before/after deep-dives
  • Example: "Why We Almost Always Recommend Navy Cabinets"

FormatFrequencyNotes

High-Performing Formats

  1. Before/After Carousel — Slide 1: dramatic "before." Slides 2–4: progress. Final slide: styled "after" + CTA.
  2. "Which would you choose?" Poll — Two options side by side. Drives comments and saves.
  3. Material Close-Up + Story — Tight texture shot + why it was chosen. Shows expertise.
  4. Design Mistake Carousel — Educational, shareable, saved.
  5. Time-Lapse Reel — Full transformation in 15–30 seconds.
