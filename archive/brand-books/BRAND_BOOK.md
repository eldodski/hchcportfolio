# 1. BRAND FOUNDATION

## 1.1 Brand Story

Hill Country Home Concepts is led by Ena, a designer with 8+ years of professional interior design experience. She's guided hundreds of homeowners through the selection process — flooring, cabinetry, countertops, fixtures, lighting, tile — and knows how to translate a half-formed vision into a cohesive, buildable design.

Ena brings systems thinking, vendor fluency, and an eye for warm, layered interiors to every project, whether it's a full home redesign or a single-room transformation. Now she's bringing that expertise directly to homeowners and real estate professionals in the Texas Hill Country and greater San Antonio area.

## 1.2 Mission Statement

To deliver warm, intentional interior design that combines creative vision with the expertise to execute flawlessly — on budget, on schedule, and always in service of real life.

## 1.3 Vision Statement

To become the leading interior design voice in the Texas Hill Country — the designer that realtors recommend to close the deal, and that homeowners call when they're ready to invest in a home that truly fits.

## 1.4 Core Values

1. **Warmth First** — Every material, every color, every choice should make a space feel inviting. Cold perfection is not the goal.
2. **Design for Real Life** — Cookbooks belong in the kitchen island. Guitars belong in the living room. Spaces are designed for the people who live in them.
3. **Intentional Quality** — Every selection is deliberate — quality finishes and carefully chosen pieces over quantity.
4. **Systems & Scale** — Great design doesn't have to mean slow or unpredictable. Production-tested processes deliver consistent results — whether it's one home or fifty.
5. **Transformation** — The best design work tells a before-and-after story. We don't decorate — we transform.
6. **Honest Partnership** — Design is collaborative — with homeowners and trades alike. Clear communication, no surprises.

## 1.5 Brand Positioning Statement

**For homeowners who want their space to feel both beautiful and genuinely livable**, Hill Country Home Concepts is a residential interior design firm that delivers warm, layered interiors grounded in rich textures, natural wood, and confident color — **unlike trend-chasing designers who prioritize Instagram over inhabitability**, HCHC designs rooms that look as good on year five as they do on day one.

## 1.6 Ideal Client Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Demographics** | Homeowners aged 30-55, household income $120K+, suburban or semi-urban |
| **Life Stage** | Settled into a home they plan to stay in; past the starter-home phase |
| **Design Taste** | Knows what they *don't* want (builder-grade beige) but needs help articulating what they *do* want |
| **Budget** | $15K-$80K per project (room or multi-room) |
| **Psychographics** | Values quality over trends, wants a professional but not intimidating experience, likely follows design accounts on Instagram but feels overwhelmed by choices |
| **Pain Point** | "I know my house could look so much better but I don't know where to start, and I'm afraid of making expensive mistakes" |

## 1.7 Tagline Options

Primary (recommended):
> **"Warm by Design."**

Alternatives:
> "Where Bold Meets Home."
> "Curated Comfort. Real Life."
> "Designed to Be Lived In."

## 2.1 Logo Direction

**Style:** Clean wordmark — "ENA DESIGN CO." in a refined serif or light sans-serif. No house icons, no furniture silhouettes, no clip art. The name IS the logo.

**Logo Suite (to be created):**
| Version | Use Case |
|---------|----------|
| **Primary Wordmark** | Website header, proposals, print materials |
| **Stacked Version** | Square formats, social media profiles |
| **Submark/Monogram** | "E" or "EDC" — favicon, watermark, small applications |
| **One-Color Versions** | White (for dark backgrounds), Espresso (for light backgrounds) |

**Logo Rules:**
- Minimum clear space: height of the "E" on all sides
- Never stretch, rotate, add drop shadows, or place on busy photography without an overlay
- Always use approved color versions (Espresso on light, Ivory on dark)

**Recommended Font for Logo:**
- **Cormorant Garamond** (Semi-Bold or Bold weight) — classical warmth, high contrast, elegant without being precious. Free on Google Fonts. Pairs perfectly with the brand aesthetic.

## 2.3 Typography System

### Primary Fonts

| Role | Font | Weight | Details |
|------|------|--------|---------|
| **Headings (H1-H2)** | **Cormorant Garamond** | SemiBold (600) | Elegant serif. Size: 36-48px web. Title case. |
| **Subheadings (H3-H4)** | **Jost** | Light (200), UPPERCASE | Wide letter-spacing (0.15-0.2em). Size: 14-18px web. The "quiet luxury" signal. |
| **Body Text** | **Jost** | Regular (400) | Clean, geometric sans-serif. Size: 16-18px web. Line height: 1.6-1.7. |
| **Accent/Captions** | **Jost** | Light Italic (200i) | Photo captions, pull quotes, subtle emphasis. |

### Typography Rules
- **Maximum 2 fonts** across all materials (Cormorant Garamond + Jost)
- Headings: always Cormorant Garamond. Never all-caps for serif headings.
- Subheadings: always Jost Light uppercase with wide tracking. This is the signature typographic treatment.
- Body: always Jost Regular. Comfortable, readable, modern.
- **Never use decorative scripts.** No cursive, no handwritten fonts. They cheapen the brand.
- Paragraph width: max 680px on web (for readability)

### Font Sources
Both fonts are **free on Google Fonts** — no licensing cost.
- Cormorant Garamond: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Cormorant+Garamond
- Jost: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Jost

## 2.4 Graphic Elements

**Borders & Dividers:**
- Thin 1px lines in Warm Sand (`#D4C5B2`) or Espresso (`#3C2A21`)
- Generous whitespace is preferred over heavy dividers

**Textures (for backgrounds/overlays):**
- Subtle linen texture at low opacity (5-10%) for warmth
- Marble texture for accent sections (ties to the Carrara countertop signature)
- NO heavy patterns, gradients, or drop shadows

**Icons:**
- Thin line-weight icons only (1-1.5px stroke)
- Style: minimal, geometric, consistent weight
- Color: Espresso or Deep Navy only

# 3. PHOTOGRAPHY & IMAGERY

## 3.1 Photography Style Guide

Photography is **the single most important brand asset** for an interior designer. It's not optional — it's the product.

### Required Photography Characteristics

| Element | Guideline |
|---------|-----------|
| **Lighting** | Natural light strongly preferred. Warm color temperature. Golden hour or overcast soft light. Supplement with warm artificial if needed. Never harsh flash. |
| **Color Temperature** | Warm. White balance should lean slightly warm (5500-6500K). Never cool/blue. |
| **Composition** | Mix of wide establishing shots (full room), medium detail shots (furniture vignettes), and tight close-ups (material textures, hardware, fabric weave). Ratio: 40/30/30. |
| **Styling** | Lived-in but curated. Books, flowers, candles, a coffee cup — signs of life. Never empty/sterile. Never cluttered. |
| **Editing** | Minimal. Clean up clutter, correct white balance, light and airy processing. No heavy filters, no extreme contrast, no desaturation. The rooms should look like they do in person. |
| **Perspective** | Eye-level or slightly above for living spaces. Straight-on for kitchens and built-ins. Avoid extreme wide-angle distortion. |

### Shot List (Per Project)

For portfolio-worthy coverage of a completed project:
1. 2-3 wide establishing shots (different angles of the main space)
2. 2-3 medium vignette shots (a styled corner, a furniture grouping)
3. 2-3 detail/texture close-ups (countertop grain, fabric texture, hardware, tile pattern)
4. 1 lifestyle shot (if possible — someone using the space naturally)
5. 1-2 architectural detail shots (lighting fixture, molding, built-in feature)

**Total: 8-14 images per project minimum.**

### Before & After Photography

Before/after is HCHC's most powerful content type. Rules:
- **Same angle, same lens** for before and after shots. This is non-negotiable for impact.
- Before shots should be honest (show the real state) but well-lit enough to read clearly
- After shots should be fully styled and professionally lit
- Always pair them — never show an after without the before for context

### Photography Investment Priority

**Immediate (do this first):**
1. Hire a professional architectural/interior photographer to reshoot the completed kitchen renovation. This single project, properly photographed and styled, is worth more than 10 phone photos. Budget: $500-$1,500 for a half-day shoot.

**Near-term:**
2. Establish a relationship with one photographer whose style aligns with the brand (warm, natural light, lifestyle-feeling). Use them for every project going forward for visual consistency.

**Ongoing:**
3. Every completed project gets a professional shoot before the client fully moves in (or with a stylist present). This is a non-negotiable cost of doing business at the portfolio-building stage.

## 3.2 Imagery Don'ts

- No phone photos in the portfolio (process/BTS social content is fine from phone)
- No photos with construction debris, moving boxes, or visible brand tags
- No heavily filtered or artificially saturated images
- No stock photography anywhere on the website — ever
- No Bassett/HGTV-branded presentation boards in the portfolio (use HCHC-branded versions)

# 4. BRAND VOICE & MESSAGING

## 4.1 Brand Voice

| Trait | What It Means | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| **Warm** | Approachable, friendly, conversational. Not stiff or formal. | "Let's talk about your space" not "Submit an inquiry regarding our services" |
| **Confident** | Knows her stuff. States opinions clearly. Not wishy-washy. | "Navy is always the right answer" not "Navy might be a nice option if you're considering it" |
| **Practical** | Grounds design in real life. Not precious or pretentious. | "This marble looks incredible AND handles a spilled glass of wine" |
| **Honest** | Direct communication. Doesn't oversell or make promises she can't keep. | "This timeline is realistic" not "We'll have your dream home in 2 weeks!" |

### Voice Do's
- Use "we" and "your" — it's a partnership
- Speak like you're talking to a smart friend, not a client file
- Use specific details (material names, color references) — they build credibility
- Be willing to have a design opinion — that's what they're hiring you for

### Voice Don'ts
- Don't use jargon without context ("we'll specify a bespoke millwork solution" → "we'll design custom built-ins")
- Don't be self-deprecating or hedging ("I think maybe this could work?")
- Don't use corporate language ("leverage," "synergy," "optimize your living experience")
- Don't use flowery clichés ("transform your space into a sanctuary of serenity")

## 4.2 Key Messages

**Elevator Pitch (10 seconds):**

# 5. WEBSITE BLUEPRINT

## 5.1 Platform Recommendation

**Primary: Showit** ($24-39/mo)
- Pixel-perfect drag-and-drop design control
- Pairs with WordPress for blog/SEO
- IDCO Studio offers interior-designer-specific templates on Showit that are designed to convert

**Alternative: Squarespace** ($23-65/mo)
- Faster to launch, less customization
- Best templates for this aesthetic: **Isabella** or **Lily**
- Good option if speed-to-market is the priority

## 5.2 Site Structure

```
HOME
├── PORTFOLIO
│   ├── [Project 1: Kitchen Renovation — "The Navy Kitchen"]
│   ├── [Project 2: Name]
│   ├── [Project 3: Name]
│   └── ... (aim for 5-8 projects at launch, add as photographed)
├── ABOUT
├── SERVICES
│   ├── Full-Service Design
│   ├── Room Refresh
│   └── Design Consultation
├── JOURNAL (blog — for SEO and content marketing)
├── CONTACT / INQUIRE
└── (footer: newsletter signup, social links, press logos when available)
```

## 5.3 Page-by-Page Guidelines

### HOME
- **Hero:** Single full-bleed image (best project photo) + tagline "Warm by Design." + CTA "View Our Work"
- **Below fold:** 3-4 portfolio preview images in a clean grid
- **Brief intro:** 2-3 sentences of positioning copy
- **Testimonial:** One strong client quote (if available)
- **CTA section:** "Ready to transform your space?" + link to Contact

### PORTFOLIO
- **Grid layout:** 2-3 columns, generous whitespace, warm hover effect (image slightly warms/zooms on hover)
- **Each project thumbnail:** Hero image + project name + project type tag (e.g., "Kitchen Renovation" / "Full Living Room")
- **Individual project pages:** 8-14 images in a curated sequence. Include:
  - Project name
  - 1-2 sentence design brief ("The homeowners wanted a kitchen that could handle a family of five and still feel like a grown-up space.")
  - Scope of work tags (e.g., "Full renovation · Cabinetry · Countertops · Flooring · Lighting")
  - Before/after comparison (if applicable — this is your strongest content)
  - No paragraphs of text — let images carry the narrative

### ABOUT
- Professional headshot of Ena (the designer) (warm, natural light, on-brand setting — ideally in a completed project)
- Brand story in first person — warm, personal, confident
- Design philosophy section
- Credentials / partnerships / certifications
- Optional: "Fun facts" or personal touch section (keeps it human)

### SERVICES
- Clear description of each service tier
- What's included at each level
- General process overview (Discovery → Concept → Design → Install → Reveal)
- **No pricing on the website.** Use "Investment starts at..." language if needed, or simply direct to inquiry.
- CTA: "Inquire" (not "Get a Quote" — signals selectivity)

### JOURNAL / BLOG
- 2-4 posts per month
- Content types: design tips, trend commentary, project behind-the-scenes, material spotlights, before/after deep-dives
- This page is your SEO engine — use keyword-rich titles
- Example posts:
  - "Why We Almost Always Recommend Navy Cabinets (And When We Don't)"
  - "Before & After: A Kitchen That Went From Builder-Grade to Custom"
  - "5 Rug Mistakes That Make Your Living Room Feel Smaller"
  - "What to Expect: Your First Design Consultation"

### CONTACT / INQUIRE
- Clean form: Name, Email, Phone, Project Type (dropdown), Timeline, Budget Range (dropdown), "Tell us about your project" (open text)
- **"Inquire"** as the page name and CTA button label
- Brief expectations copy: "We typically respond within 48 hours..."
- Optional: FAQ section addressing common questions (budget, timeline, location)

## 5.4 Website Design Rules

- **Backgrounds:** Ivory Warm (`#F5F0EB`) or white. Never dark backgrounds for main content areas.
- **Text:** Espresso (`#3C2A21`) for body. Deep Navy (`#1B2A4A`) for headings or emphasis.
- **Buttons:** Deep Navy background + Ivory Warm text. Hover: Mocha (`#A37762`) background.
- **Images:** Always full-width or large. Never small thumbnails on the main pages.
- **Whitespace:** Generous. When in doubt, add more space.
- **Animations:** Subtle only. Gentle fade-in on scroll. No bouncing, sliding, or spinning elements.
- **Mobile:** Must be flawless. 60%+ of traffic will be mobile. Test every page on phone.

# 6. SOCIAL MEDIA STRATEGY

## 6.1 Platform Priority

| Priority | Platform | Role |
|----------|----------|------|
| **1 (Primary)** | **Instagram** | Portfolio showcase, client acquisition, brand building |
| **2 (Secondary)** | **Pinterest** | Long-tail SEO, inspiration discovery, passive traffic to website |
| **3 (When ready)** | **Blog/Journal** | SEO anchor, authority building, content repurposing source |

Focus on Instagram first. Do it well before expanding.

## 6.2 Instagram Strategy

### Content Pillars & Posting Mix

| Pillar | % of Content | Content Types |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Portfolio / Reveals** | 40% | Final project photos, before/after carousels, room reveals |
| **Education / Authority** | 25% | Design tips, material guides, trend commentary, "why I chose this" |
| **Behind the Scenes** | 20% | Site visits, material shopping, mood boards, design process |
| **Personal / Lifestyle** | 15% | Design inspiration travel, favorite local spots, day-in-the-life |

### Posting Cadence

| Format | Frequency | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Feed Posts** | 4-5/week | Mix of carousels, single images, and Reels |
| **Carousels** | 2-3/week | Highest engagement format (1.92% avg). Prioritize these. |
| **Reels** | 1-2/week | Time-lapses, room tours, quick tips |
| **Stories** | Daily | Polls, Q&As, casual BTS, reposts of inspiration |

### High-Performing Post Formats

1. **Before/After Carousel** — THE highest-performing format for interior designers. Slide 1: dramatic "before." Slides 2-4: process/progress. Final slide: styled "after" + CTA.
2. **"Which would you choose?" Poll** — Two design options side by side. Drives comments and saves.
3. **Material Close-Up + Story** — Tight shot of a texture/material + caption explaining why it was chosen. Shows expertise.
4. **Design Mistake Carousel** — "5 mistakes making your living room feel smaller" — educational, shareable, saves.
5. **Time-Lapse Reel** — Full room transformation compressed to 15-30 seconds. Hypnotic. Shareable.

### Hashtag Strategy
- **Maximum 3-5 hashtags per post** (the algorithm has changed — less is more)
- Use niche-specific, not generic:
  - YES: `#navykitchen` `#kitchenrenovation` `#warminteriors` `#transitionaldesign` `#beforeandafterhome`
  - NO: `#interiordesign` `#homedecor` `#beautiful` `#inspo`
- Rotate hashtags — don't use the same set every time
- Put hashtags in the caption, not comments (algorithm change 2025)

### Instagram Aesthetic / Grid

- **Color consistency:** Warm tones throughout. No cold/blue-toned images breaking the grid.
- **Alternating rhythm:** Wide room shot → detail/texture close-up → lifestyle/BTS → repeat
- **No heavy text graphics** on the grid. If educational content needs text, put it inside carousels (slide 2+), not on the cover slide.
- Cover slide of carousels: always a beautiful image, never a text-heavy graphic

## 6.3 Pinterest Strategy

- **Pin frequency:** 3-5 pins/day (use a scheduler like Tailwind)
- **Pin every portfolio image** individually with keyword-rich descriptions
- **Create boards** that mirror how clients search: "Kitchen Renovation Ideas," "Navy Kitchen Design," "Living Room Makeover," "Warm Neutral Interiors," "Transitional Home Design"
- **Blog posts → Pinterest:** Every blog article becomes 3-5 pins with different images
- Pinterest is a search engine, not a social network — write descriptions like you're writing for Google

## 6.4 Content Repurposing System

One completed project should generate:

```
1 Project Shoot (8-14 photos)
    → 1 Blog post (project story + photos + design decisions)
    → 3-4 Instagram feed posts (hero shots, detail shots)
    → 1-2 Before/after carousels
    → 1 Reel (room tour or time-lapse)
    → 5-8 Pinterest pins (individual images with keyword descriptions)
    → 3-5 Stories (BTS process, client reaction, material close-ups)
    → 1 Newsletter feature
```

**This means one project = 15-25 pieces of content.** That's 2-4 weeks of social media from a single shoot.

# 8. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

## 8.1 Where HCHC Sits in the Market

```
                    TRADITIONAL ←————————————→ MODERN
                         |                        |
    ACCESSIBLE     Joanna Gaines            Studio McGee
    (Mass market)        |                     ★ HCHC ★
                         |                        |
    PREMIUM        Rose Uniacke            Vincent Van Duysen
    (High-end)           |                        |
                    TRADITIONAL ←————————————→ MODERN
```

HCHC's sweet spot: **accessible-to-mid-market, leaning modern-transitional.** Same general zone as Studio McGee, but with more warmth and personality (less catalog-perfect, more real-life). This is the largest addressable market in residential design.

## 8.2 Differentiators to Own

1. **Navy as a Signature** — Claim it explicitly. Make it part of the visual brand, the content, the projects. "The designer who made me fall in love with navy" is a referral line worth building toward.

2. **Before/After Storytelling** — The kitchen renovation proves HCHC can take a space from dated to dramatic. This transformation narrative is the single most engaging content type in interior design. Lead with it.

3. **Renovation + Design (Not Just Decorating)** — Many designers only do furniture selection. HCHC does structural renovation, material specification, and contractor coordination. That's a higher-value service worth highlighting.

4. **Real Life, Not Showroom** — The cookbooks in the island, the personalized touches. This is a genuine differentiator against designers whose work looks beautiful but unlived-in.
